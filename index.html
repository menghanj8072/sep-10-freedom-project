What is animation and what is it used for?

Animation is a method of using still images, models, or even puppets to create an illusion of movement in a sequence. Animations can be used anywhere. In education, Animation can educate the audience through entertainment or sometimes animation has the ability to transfer emotion efficiently. Animation lets you show your thoughts, ideas, and concepts in various fields like marketing, advertising, and business.

How many types of animations are there?

There are 5 types of animations: Traditional animation, 2d animation, 3d animation, motion graphics, and stop motion. So what are the differences between these types of animations?

  • Traditional animation or cel animation is the oldest form of animation. In traditional animation, in order to create an animated sequence, the amator has to draw every frame on a celluloid transparent paper. It works the same way as a flipbook
  • 2D animation or two dimensional animation, is a mixture of artistic methods and media design that produces the illusion of flow in a two-dimensional environment.
  • 3D animation or CGI is the most common one seen today. In 3D animation, animators use programs on computers to create 3D characters and move the characterâ€™s body part around. The animator has to set digital frames every time when the characters are in the right position. Then the computer will calculate the motion from each of the frames.
  • Motion graphics animation is a type of animation mostly used in business. Motion graphics are generally used in bigger multimedia projects with larger scopes and images and texts are animated to represent an illusion of movement.
  • Stop motion animation also known as stop-frame animation that is created by combining a sequence of individually illustrated frames. This makes the frame appear to display independent motion.
